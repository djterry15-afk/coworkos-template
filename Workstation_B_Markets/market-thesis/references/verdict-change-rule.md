# Verdict-Change Rule
*(Example settled rule.) Project-tier reference (Layer 3) — loaded by trigger, not every session. It lives
here, not in `CONTEXT.md`, because it is **settled and separable** from the current verdict (which is live
state). This file is the template's worked example of when a Reasoning Workspace earns a `references/` folder.*

---

## The rule

The thesis verdict in `CONTEXT.md` is the live state; *how it is allowed to change* is this standing rule.

- The verdict moves only on **confirmed** evidence: a graded brief from `research-factory/` that clears the
  reliability bar, or a `stress-monitor/` metric crossing a **pre-declared** threshold. A single unconfirmed
  reading is *input to reasoning*, not a verdict change.
- Every change records its trigger — which brief or which threshold — with a `_reasoning-log/` distillation
  that traces the reasoning. No silent edits to the verdict.
- The **direction** (the overall stance) changes only at a **phase boundary**: a regime shift confirmed by
  more than one independent metric, never a single noisy series.

## Why this is a rule, not state

The *current* verdict belongs in `CONTEXT.md` — it changes as evidence arrives. The *standard for changing
it* belongs here — it rarely changes, and the reasoning leans on it every time. That split (a settled rule
extracted from live state) is exactly when a Reasoning Workspace earns a `references/` folder.

The boundary, stated as a test: **detailed-but-unsettled exploration is not a rule — it stays in
`CONTEXT.md`.** Only a convention that has stopped moving, and that the reasoning repeatedly leans on, earns
separation into `references/`. Until then, the folder stays absent (an empty `references/` is the
over-engineering trap, not a head start).
