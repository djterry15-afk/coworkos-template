## 1. Question
"When financial stress hits, which market metrics move *first*, and which only confirm the move after it is
already underway?"

## 2. State of the evidence
The (placeholder) evidence **leans toward a lead/lag ordering** but with an important caveat. The stronger
source describes funding-plumbing metrics moving weeks ahead of credit spreads; a second source shows the
lead time is episode-dependent and can collapse to near-zero. The body of evidence supports *"funding
plumbing tends to lead, credit confirms — but the lead time is not guaranteed."*

## 3. Evidence FOR (funding plumbing leads)
- Term premium + secured–unsecured funding spread widened several weeks before HY spreads moved *(source 1,
  A, printed figure)*. Credit OAS widened only after funding had tightened *(source 1, A, printed figure)*.

## 4. Evidence AGAINST / complicating
- In at least one episode, funding and credit stress moved **near-simultaneously** — the lead is
  episode-dependent, not a fixed law *(source 2, B, chart-read)*.

## 5. Contradictions & gaps
- Sources 1 and 2 disagree on whether the lead time is reliable. What would resolve it: a larger sample of
  episodes with consistent timing measurement, which neither placeholder source fully provides. Missing:
  cross-currency basis and Treasury-depth as additional early signals (flagged for a future run).
