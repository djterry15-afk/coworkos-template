# Research Brief Format — _config (L3, stable across runs)
*The 03_synthesize output schema. The brief lays out **evidence**; it does not deliver a
verdict. The verdict is the operator's, in the parent market-thesis project `CONTEXT.md`.*

---

## File: `03_synthesize/output/brief-<slug>.md`

## Sections

1. **Question** — the seed, verbatim.
2. **State of the evidence** *(one paragraph)* — what the *body of evidence* supports or
   contests, and its weight (strong / mixed / thin). Describe the **evidence**, not the
   conclusion. Not "the thesis is X."
3. **Evidence FOR** — each item: claim · source (title, grade, authorship) · verbatim quote or
   exact datum · provenance pointer into the snapshot.
4. **Evidence AGAINST / complicating** — same structure.
5. **Contradictions & gaps** — where graded sources disagree · what would resolve it · what's
   missing.
6. **Source ledger** — every source: grade · authorship · state · how-found. The audit trail.
7. **Verdict: deferred** — one line: the call is the operator's → `CONTEXT.md`. *Optionally* the
   candidate angle the evidence points to, flagged as **the agent's read, for the operator to accept
   or reject** — never asserted.

---

## Rules

- **No claim without a graded source + provenance.** If it's not in the snapshot, it's not in
  the brief.
- **C-grade and ai-authored material never appear as evidence** — only, if at all, as a noted lead.
- **Stop short of the verdict.** An angle may be *offered* (gate 01 surfaced it); it is never
  *concluded*. Evidence in, evidence out.
- **Don't smooth contradictions.** Surfacing them is the brief's value.
