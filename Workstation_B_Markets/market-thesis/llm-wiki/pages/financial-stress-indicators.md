## The lead/lag structure

When system-wide financial stress builds, the evidence (placeholder) points to a consistent ordering:

- **Funding plumbing moves first.** The term premium and the secured-vs-unsecured overnight funding spread
  (repo vs. fed funds) widen *before* broad risk repricing — they are the earliest legible sign that
  funding is getting harder. *(brief sources 1–2, grades A/B.)*
- **Credit spreads confirm, and lag.** High-yield and investment-grade OAS widen *after* the funding signal,
  as risk appetite drops — they tell you the stress is real, but later. *(brief source 1, grade B.)*

**Caveat (source 2, grade A):** the lead time is **episode-dependent** — in at least one studied episode
funding and credit stress moved nearly together. The ordering is a tendency, not a law.
