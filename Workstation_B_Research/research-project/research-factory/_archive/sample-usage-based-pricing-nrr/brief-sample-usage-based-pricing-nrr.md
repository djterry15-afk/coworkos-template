## 1. Question
"When a SaaS company switches from seat-based to usage-based pricing, does NRR rise or fall in year one,
and what drives the difference?"

## 2. State of the evidence
The (placeholder) evidence is **mixed**. One benchmark points to a short dip then recovery; one stronger,
peer-reviewed source shows the outcome depends heavily on customer usage patterns, with some segments
declining and not recovering. The body of evidence supports *"it depends on customer mix"* more than a
single directional answer.

## 3. Evidence FOR (dip-then-recover)
- Median NRR fell ~6 pts for two quarters, then exceeded baseline by Q4 *(source 1, B, printed figure)*.

## 4. Evidence AGAINST / complicating
- Seasonal-usage accounts saw **sustained** NRR declines, not recovery *(source 2, A, printed figure)* —
  the strongest source, and it refuses the clean dip-then-recover story.

## 5. Contradictions & gaps
- Sources 1 and 2 disagree on whether the dip recovers. What would resolve it: NRR broken out **by customer
  usage profile**, which neither placeholder source fully provides. Missing: cohort-level data over ≥6
  quarters.
