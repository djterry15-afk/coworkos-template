# Reliability Bar — _config (L3, stable across runs)
*Applied at 01_source. Two **independent** axes: GRADE (how much weight) and AUTHORSHIP
(who wrote it). Grade ⊥ location — a source's grade is about credibility, not where it was found.*

---

## Grade — credibility weight

| Grade | What qualifies | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Primary source or named expert with a track record: original data (Fed / BIS / Treasury / BLS series), institutional research, a named author's book or paper (e.g. Anderson, *Bonds and Debt*), official filings | Citable as direct evidence — quote + provenance |
| **B** | Reputable secondary analysis: established financial press, named analysts, think-tank notes that cite their own sources | Citable, but attribute as secondary; prefer to trace to the A-source it rests on |
| **C** | Anonymous / blog / forum / unattributed, marketing, or anything whose provenance can't be established | **Leads only.** Not citable as evidence. Use to find an A/B source, then cite that |

---

## Authorship tag — a separate axis, always recorded

- **human-primary** — written by a named human or institution. Citable per its grade.
- **ai-authored / ai-summarized** — LLM-generated or AI-summarized content (including your own
  past ChatGPT dumps). **Mine for substance and leads; never cite as evidence.** Downgrade to a
  pointer: trace to the underlying primary source and cite *that*.

---

## Settled vs live — fan-in guard 6

Prefer **settled** sources: published, dated, done moving. A **live** source (a mid-flight
synthesis, a thesis still being argued, a number that changes hourly) is a
**stop-and-reconsider** at the gate — if you need live material, the question may not be ready
to research. Record each source's state in the manifest.

---

## The bar is a gate, not an automaton

Grading is the **human's call** at 01_source. This file gives the rubric; the operator
approves the grades. The factory structures the reliability judgment — it does not make it.
